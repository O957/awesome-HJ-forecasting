# Awesome Human Judgment Forecasting [![Awesome](https://awesome.re/badge-flat.svg)](https://awesome.re)

_A curated[^curation_details] list of awesome resources on human judgment forecasting[^definition_human_judgment_forecasting]._

[^definition_human_judgment_forecasting]: (from Wikipedia, see citation at the bottom) **Forecasting** is the process of making predictions about the future based on past and present data. Human judgment forecasting involves making probabilistic predictions about future events using systematic methods, calibrated probability assessments, and aggregation techniques. Research has shown that properly trained forecasters using structured approaches can outperform domain experts, prediction markets, and even intelligence analysts with classified information. The field draws on cognitive psychology, decision science, and behavioral economics to understand and improve predictive accuracy. CITATION: Wikipedia contributors. "Forecasting." Wikipedia. <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forecasting>.

## Forecasting Platforms

### Public Forecasting Platforms

1. [Good Judgment Open](https://www.gjopen.com/): The world's largest public forecasting platform for making probabilistic forecasts on geopolitical, economic, and societal questions. Operated by Good Judgment Inc, emerged from the Good Judgment Project that won IARPA's forecasting tournament.
2. [Metaculus](https://www.metaculus.com/): Reputation-based forecasting platform with 1M+ predictions covering AI progress, biosecurity, nuclear security, and climate change. Uses sophisticated aggregation algorithms and hosts tournaments with monetary prizes.
3. [Manifold Markets](https://manifold.markets/): Play-money prediction market platform using virtual currency with a wide variety of questions across politics, sports, technology, and entertainment.
4. [Futuur](https://futuur.com/): Forecasting platform focusing on long-term questions and calibration training, with emphasis on accuracy tracking and skill development.

### Professional/Commercial Platforms

1. [Good Judgment Analytics](https://goodjudgment.com/services/analytics/): Professional forecasting services using Superforecasters for corporate and government clients, offering custom forecasting tournaments and consulting.
2. [INFER](https://www.infer-pub.com/): Public version of Cultivate Labs forecasting platform with questions on science, technology, geopolitics, and policy. Combines human judgment with machine learning.

### Specialized/Academic

1. [PredictionBook](https://predictionbook.com/): Simple open-source personal forecasting journal for recording predictions, getting resolution reminders, and tracking calibration over time.
2. [Replication Markets](https://www.replicationmarkets.com/): Academic forecasting platform predicting which social science studies will replicate, run as a collaboration between DARPA and academic researchers.

## Books & Core Literature

### Foundational Books

1. [Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction](https://www.amazon.com/Superforecasting-Science-Prediction-Philip-Tetlock/dp/0804136718): Philip E. Tetlock & Dan Gardner (2015). The definitive book on superforecasting, documenting findings from the Good Judgment Project and identifying traits of top forecasters.
2. [Expert Political Judgment: How Good Is It? How Can We Know?](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691128719/expert-political-judgment): Philip E. Tetlock (2005). Landmark 20-year study tracking expert predictions, finding experts barely better than chance and introducing the fox vs. hedgehog distinction.
3. [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374533557/thinkingfastandslow): Daniel Kahneman (2011). Nobel laureate's synthesis of judgment and decision-making research covering System 1 (fast/intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow/analytical) thinking and cognitive biases affecting forecasts.
4. [The Signal and the Noise: Why So Many Predictions Fail -- But Some Don't](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/305826/the-signal-and-the-noise-by-nate-silver/): Nate Silver (2012). Explores prediction across domains including politics, weather, baseball, and economics, emphasizing Bayesian thinking and probabilistic reasoning.

### Advanced/Specialized

1. [Simple Heuristics That Make Us Smart](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/simple-heuristics-that-make-us-smart-9780195143812): Gerd Gigerenzer et al. (1999). Demonstrates how simple decision rules can outperform complex models, offering an alternative perspective to the heuristics-and-biases view.
2. [Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment](https://www.littlebrown.com/titles/daniel-kahneman/noise/9780316451406/): Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein (2021). Examines unwanted variability in human judgment with relevance to forecast consistency and aggregation methods.
3. [Risk Savvy: How to Make Good Decisions](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/313832/risk-savvy-by-gerd-gigerenzer/): Gerd Gigerenzer (2014). Practical guide to understanding probability and risk, emphasizing natural frequencies over probabilities for better intuitions.
4. [How to Measure Anything: Finding the Value of Intangibles in Business](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/How+to+Measure+Anything%3A+Finding+the+Value+of+Intangibles+in+Business%2C+3rd+Edition-p-9781118539279): Douglas W. Hubbard (3rd ed, 2014). Includes extensive coverage of calibrated probability assessment, Fermi estimation, and decomposition techniques.

## Training & Skill Development

### Formal Training Programs

1. [Good Judgment Superforecasting Fundamentals](https://good-judgment.thinkific.com/courses/Superforecasting-Fundamentals): Self-paced 90-minute online course co-developed by Philip Tetlock covering probabilistic reasoning, base rates, calibration, and cognitive biases.
2. [Good Judgment Workshops](https://goodjudgment.com/services/training/): In-person or virtual workshops led by Superforecasters and researchers, customizable for organizations with interactive small group practice.
3. [Calibrated Probability Assessments](https://hubbardresearch.com/shop/calibrated-probability-assessments/): Hubbard Decision Research training that teaches how to assess subjective probabilities measurably, including 80% confidence interval training.

### Practice & Calibration Tools

1. [Calibrate Your Judgment](https://www.clearerthinking.org/post/2019/10/16/practice-making-accurate-predictions-with-our-new-tool): ClearerThinking.org free interactive tool for practicing calibrated predictions with immediate feedback and improvement tracking.
2. [Betting on Things](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/K5CKmGudZJHnDYmvG/betting-on-things): LessWrong guide to using peer-to-peer bets as a calibration training mechanism, emphasizing putting "skin in the game."

### Books on Technique

1. [Sharpening Your Forecasting Skills](https://rdl.train.army.mil/catalog-ws/view/ARI_TinT_WBT/references/Superforecaster.pdf): U.S. Army Research Institute training document synthesizing Tetlock's findings for military applications with focus on the forecast-measure-revise cycle.

## Research Organizations & Institutes

### Leading Research Centers

1. [Forecasting Research Institute (FRI)](https://forecastingresearch.org/): Led by Philip Tetlock as Chief Scientist, developing forecasting methods for high-stakes decisions. Recent projects include LEAP, ForecastBench, and biosecurity risk forecasting.
2. [Good Judgment Inc](https://goodjudgment.com/): Commercial entity founded by Tetlock and Mellers that emerged from the Good Judgment Project, providing Superforecaster services to government, nonprofits, and corporations.
3. [Decision Research](https://www.decision-research.org/): Eugene, Oregon research institute founded by Paul Slovic studying judgment, decision-making, and risk perception, with contributions to probability estimation and risk communication.
4. [Center for Advanced Hindsight](https://advanced-hindsight.com/): Duke University center led by Dan Ariely conducting behavioral economics research including decision-making and forecasting, with focus on translating research to practice.

### University Programs

1. [H.O. Stekler Research Program on Forecasting](https://cer.columbian.gwu.edu/ho-stekler-research-program-forecasting): George Washington University program supporting research, teaching, and dissertations in forecasting.
2. [International Institute of Forecasters (IIF)](https://forecasters.org/): Non-profit founded in 1981 that organizes the annual International Symposium on Forecasting, publishes the International Journal of Forecasting and Foresight, and sponsors M Competitions.
3. [Berkeley D-Lab Forecasting Research](https://dlab.berkeley.edu/news/predicting-future-harnessing-power-probabilistic-judgements-through-forecasting-tournaments): UC Berkeley research on forecasting tournaments and collective intelligence, studying determinants of accurate forecasters and optimal tournament design.

### Government/Intelligence

1. [IARPA (Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Activity)](https://www.iarpa.gov/): U.S. intelligence research agency that sponsored the ACE tournament (2011-2015) won by the Good Judgment Project and currently runs the ForeST program for S&T forecasting.
2. [RAND Corporation - Forecasting Research](https://www.rand.org/topics/forecasting.html): Research on geopolitical forecasting and decision support using Superforecaster methods.

## Academic Journals

### Forecasting-Specific Journals

1. [International Journal of Forecasting](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/international-journal-of-forecasting): Leading journal in forecasting and official publication of IIF, covering economic, marketing, financial, and demographic forecasting with double-blind peer review.
2. [Journal of Forecasting](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/1099131x): Multidisciplinary journal covering statistics, economics, psychology, and engineering, published by Wiley.
3. [Foresight: The International Journal of Applied Forecasting](https://foresight.forecasters.org/): IIF publication focused on practical applications that bridges the gap between academic research and practitioner needs.

### Related Journals

1. [Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Processes](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/organizational-behavior-and-human-decision-processes): Publishes research on judgment and decision-making relevant to understanding forecaster psychology and organizational forecasting.
2. [Judgment and Decision Making](https://journal.sjdm.org/): Open-access journal of the Society for Judgment and Decision Making covering cognitive biases, probability judgment, and risk perception.
3. [Management Science](https://pubsonline.informs.org/journal/mnsc): INFORMS journal covering operations research, forecasting methodology, and decision analysis.

## Software & Tools

### Forecasting-Specific Software

1. [IARPA ACE Program](https://www.iarpa.gov/research-programs/ace): Aggregative Contingent Estimation program (2011-2015) that tested forecasting tournament designs and aggregation algorithms, producing foundational research on superforecasting methodology.
2. [Metaculus Aggregation Algorithm](https://www.metaculus.com/help/faq/#metaculus-prediction): Proprietary algorithm combining median with performance-based weighting, accounting for recency, track record, and prediction timing.

### Probability & Statistics Tools

1. [Guesstimate](https://www.getguesstimate.com/): Web-based Monte Carlo modeling for uncertain quantities with a visual interface for probability distributions. Free and open-source.
2. [Squiggle](https://www.squiggle-language.com/): Probabilistic programming language for estimation developed by the Quantified Uncertainty Research Institute, serving as a lightweight alternative to complex statistical software.
3. [Python scipy.stats](https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/stats.html): Comprehensive statistics library including probability distributions, Brier score calculation, and calibration assessment tools.
4. [R forecast package](https://pkg.robjhyndman.com/forecast/): Time series forecasting in R by Rob Hyndman (Monash University), including ARIMA, exponential smoothing, and accuracy metrics.

### Calibration Assessment

1. [Python sklearn.calibration](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/calibration.html): Machine learning calibration tools including calibration curves, Platt scaling, and isotonic regression for assessing probabilistic predictions.
2. [Brier Score Calculator](https://goodjudgment.com/brier-score-calculator/): Simple web calculator from Good Judgment Inc for computing Brier scores from forecasts and outcomes.

## Prediction Markets

### Operational Prediction Markets

1. [Polymarket](https://polymarket.com/): Largest prediction market by volume, blockchain-based (Polygon) with cryptocurrency (USDC) trading across politics, sports, crypto, and entertainment.
2. [Kalshi](https://kalshi.com/): CFTC-regulated prediction market allowing USD trading on permitted event types including economic indicators, weather, and elections.
3. [PredictIt](https://www.predictit.org/): Academic research project operated by Victoria University of Wellington focusing on U.S. politics (CFTC no-action relief withdrawn August 2023; operating in wind-down).
4. [Iowa Electronic Markets (IEM)](https://iemweb.biz.uiowa.edu/): Longest-running prediction market (since 1988) operated by the University of Iowa with real money and investment caps, focused on U.S. elections.

### Corporate/Internal Markets

1. [Cultivate Labs](https://www.cultivatelabs.com/): Enterprise forecasting platform combining prediction markets with polling, used by Fortune 500 companies and government agencies with support for custom tournaments.
2. [Almanis](https://www.almanis.io/): Corporate forecasting platform providing internal prediction markets for business decisions with enterprise system integration.

### Research & Data

1. [PredictIt Data](https://www.predictit.org/api/): API access to market prices and trading data useful for research on market accuracy and dynamics, with historical data available.
2. [Insight Prediction](https://insightprediction.com/): Prediction market aggregator and analytics platform tracking multiple platforms and providing meta-analysis of market predictions.

## Communities & Forums

### Online Communities

1. [LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/): Rationality community with extensive forecasting discussions, quarterly prediction contests, and focus on calibration training and Bayesian reasoning.
2. [Effective Altruism Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/): Discussions on forecasting for cause prioritization and impact assessment, including long-term forecasting of existential risks and AI timelines.
3. [Good Judgment Open Community](https://www.gjopen.com/comments): Discussion forums attached to forecasting questions where forecasters share reasoning, news updates, and analysis.
4. [Metaculus Comments](https://www.metaculus.com/questions/): Question-specific discussion threads with high-quality analysis and prediction rationale from top forecasters.

### Subreddits

1. [r/forecasting](https://www.reddit.com/r/forecasting/): General forecasting subreddit covering time series, judgment-based, and hybrid methods.
2. [The Motte](https://www.themotte.org/): Discussion community (formerly r/TheMotte) with forecasting threads emphasizing intellectual rigor and charitable interpretation.

### Professional Networks

1. [Society for Judgment and Decision Making (SJDM)](https://www.sjdm.org/): Academic society for JDM researchers with an annual conference, job postings, and publications open to students and practitioners.
2. [International Institute of Forecasters LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/international-institute-of-forecasters/): Professional network for forecasters with job postings, conference updates, and research discussions.

## Courses & Educational Resources

### University Courses

1. [Forecasting: Principles and Practice (3rd ed)](https://otexts.com/fpp3/): Free online textbook by Rob Hyndman & George Athanasopoulos with comprehensive coverage of time series forecasting with R code (Monash University).
2. [Judgmental Forecasting](https://qiushiyan.github.io/fpp/judgmental-forecasts.html): Textbook chapter covering three settings for judgmental forecasts -- no data, adjusting statistical forecasts, and combining independent forecasts.

### Online Courses

1. [Forecasting Methods and Principles (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/forecasting-methods): University-level course covering quantitative and qualitative methods including judgment-based approaches.

### Video Resources

1. [IARPA Geopolitical Forecasting Tournaments](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EJL8vX3a9n8): INFORMS talk providing a 10-year overview of ACE and HFC tournaments and behavioral research contributions to intelligence forecasting.
2. [Philip Tetlock - Edge Master Class: A Short Course in Superforecasting](https://www.edge.org/conversation/philip_tetlock-edge-master-class-2015-a-short-course-in-superforecasting-class-i): Video series and transcript covering granularity, crowd wisdom, and extremizing algorithms with Q&A featuring Daniel Kahneman.

### Interactive Training

1. [80,000 Hours Podcast Episodes on Forecasting](https://80000hours.org/podcast/): Interviews with forecasting researchers and practitioners with deep dives into methods, career applications, and AI timelines.

### Articles & Guides

1. [Ten Commandments for Aspiring Superforecasters](https://fs.blog/ten-commandments-for-superforecasters/): Farnam Street distillation of key principles including triaging, breaking down problems, and balancing inside/outside views.
2. [Superforecasting Tips and Techniques](https://www.thendobetter.com/investing/2019/7/13/superforecasting-tips): Practical compilation of techniques emphasizing fox vs. hedgehog thinking, deliberate practice, and error-balancing.
3. [Forecasting Advice from Good Judgment Open](https://www.gjopen.com/comments/1165014): Community-compiled best practices covering question selection, research strategies, updating frequency, and progressive overload.

## People

- [Philip Tetlock](https://mgmt.wharton.upenn.edu/profile/tetlock/) - University of Pennsylvania. Foundational figure in superforecasting, author of "Expert Political Judgment" and "Superforecasting," co-founded the Good Judgment Project.
- [Barbara Mellers](https://psychology.sas.upenn.edu/people/barbara-mellers) - University of Pennsylvania. Co-PI of the Good Judgment Project, led large-scale field experiments on boosting forecast accuracy.
- [Rob Hyndman](https://github.com/robjhyndman) - Monash University. Creator of the forecast R package and co-author of "Forecasting: Principles and Practice."
- [Gerd Gigerenzer](https://www.gerd-gigerenzer.com/) - Max Planck Institute for Human Development. Pioneered research on fast and frugal heuristics, demonstrating simple decision rules can outperform complex models.
- [Tom McAndrew](https://github.com/tomcm39) - Lehigh University. Develops ensemble algorithms combining computational models with human judgment for infectious disease forecasting.
- [Ville Satopaa](https://github.com/satopaa) - INSEAD. Develops theory and methodology for probability aggregation and extremization of forecasts.
- [Lyle Ungar](https://github.com/ungarl) - University of Pennsylvania. Member of the Good Judgment Project, bridges machine learning and NLP with human judgment for the IARPA ACE program.
- [Daniel Kahneman](https://kahneman.scholar.princeton.edu/) - Princeton University (1934-2024). Nobel Prize in Economics (2002) for work on judgment under uncertainty. Author of "Thinking, Fast and Slow."
